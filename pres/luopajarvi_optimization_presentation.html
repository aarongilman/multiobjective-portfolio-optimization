Multiobjective portfolio optimization¶

Antti Luopajärvi¶

1. Objectives in general¶

There are six objectives for portfolio:

  1. Maximize expected return.
  2. Maximize environmental, social, and corporate- sustainability.
  3. Maximize dividend yield.
  4. Maximize clean energy use.
  5. Minimize price-earnings ratio.
  6. Optimize volatility.

2. Decision variables¶

Using proportional amount of investment into a company $i$ as decision variable $x_i$.

In total 361 companies.

3. Objective functions¶

The objective functions could be then modelled as $$ \begin{align} \text{maximize } &\{\sum_{i=1}^{n} r_ix_i,\sum_{i=1}^{n} s_ix_i,\sum_{i=1}^{n} d_ix_i,\sum_{i=1}^{n} c_ix_i,-\sum_{i=1}^{n} p_ix_i\}\\ \text{optimize } &\sum_{i=1}^{n} \beta_ix_i\\ \end{align} $$

where $r_i, s_i, d_i, c_i, p_i, \beta_i$ are expected return for investment, ESG-sustainability, dividend yield, clean energy use, price-earnings-ratio, and beta for company $i$, respectively.

  1. Variable for expected return, $r_i$, is calculated as $$ \begin{align} r_i &= R_{f_m} + \beta_i(ER_m-R_{f_m}),\\ \text{where}\\ R_{f_m} &= \text{ the risk free-rate for market m},\\ \beta_i &= \text{ beta coefficient for company i},\\ ER_m &= \text{ expected average return on market m}.\\ \end{align}\\ $$
  2. For $s_i$(ESG-sustainability), $d_i$(dividend yield), and $p_i$(price-earnings), the values are those of corresponding attribute values in data for company $i$.
  3. For $c_i$(clean energy use), variable value is calculated as a sum of attributes 'Clean200' and 'ScienceBasedTargets' for company $i$.
  4. For optimizing volatility, portfolio beta can be aimed to be equal to 1. In this case, the volatility of the portfolio is same as the market's.

4. Constraints¶

  1. Sum of weights should be equal to one.

  2. For diversifying portfolio, weight of a single asset should not be more than some value, e.g. 0.1.

$$ \begin{align} &\sum_{i=1}^n x_i = 1\\ &0 \leq x_i \leq 0.1, i = 1,..,n \end{align} $$

5. Potential approaches to solve the problem¶

I have done this far:

  1. Transform objective $$ \begin{align} \text{optimize } &\sum_{i=1}^{n} \beta_ix_i\\ \end{align} $$ into a pair of inequality constraints, so that $$ \begin{align} &\sum_{i=1}^{n} \beta_ix_i + \epsilon \geq 1\\ &\sum_{i=1}^{n} \beta_ix_i - \epsilon \leq 1\\ \end{align} $$ where $\epsilon$ is a small value, e.g. 0.1, to relax the constraints.

  2. Solving the problem with epsilon constraint method, using 1) expected return and 2) clean energy use as objectives.
